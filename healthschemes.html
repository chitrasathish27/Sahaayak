National Health Protection Scheme (NHPS)

The National Health Protection Scheme (NHPS), also known as Pradhan Mantri Jan Arogya Yojana (PM-JAY), is a government-sponsored insurance program in India that aims to provide quality healthcare to poor and vulnerable families. The scheme provides coverage for hospitalization expenses and transportation charges, and it targets 50 crore individuals, with a family cover of Rs 5 lakhs per family per year. The beneficiary households under the NHPS are based on the deprivation and occupation status drawn from the Socioeconomic and Caste Census database.

1.Coverage for Hospitalization Expenses: The scheme provides coverage for hospitalization expenses, including secondary and tertiary care, ensuring that beneficiaries have access to necessary medical treatments and procedures.
2. Financial Protection: By offering a family cover of Rs 5 lakhs per year, the NHPS aims to provide financial protection against catastrophic health expenditures, reducing the burden of out-of-pocket payments for healthcare services
3. Comprehensive Care: The scheme covers almost all secondary care and most tertiary care, including free essential drugs and diagnostic services, ensuring that beneficiaries have access to a wide range of medical services
4. Targeted Support: The NHPS aims to support over 10 crore economically disadvantaged individuals, including those considered deprived as per the Socio Economic and Caste Census (SECC) criteria, thereby targeting those in need of healthcare assistance the most
5. Access to Public and Private Facilities: Beneficiaries can avail benefits in both public and empaneled private facilities, ensuring access to a network of healthcare providers

1. Socio-Economic Criteria: The scheme primarily targets economically vulnerable families.
2. SECC Database: The eligibility for NHPS is often determined using the Socio-Economic and Caste Census (SECC) database.
3. Rural and Urban Focus: Both rural and urban families falling under the specified socio-economic criteria are eligible for the benefits of NHPS.
4. Family Unit: The scheme covers families rather than individuals.
5. No Age Bar: There is no age criterion for eligibility under the scheme. All family members, irrespective of age, are covered.

2. Generation of e-cards: Once identified, eligible families are issued a unique identification card (e-card) that contains their details
3. Hospital Empanelment: Participating hospitals and healthcare providers are empaneled under the scheme. Beneficiaries can receive treatment at these empaneled hospitals.
4. Treatment and Coverage: When a beneficiary seeks medical treatment at an empaneled hospital, the cost of the treatment is covered under the NHPS.
5. Claim Process: The empaneled hospital submits the claim to the insurance agency responsible for the NHPS .

Central Government Health Scheme (CGHS)

It is a health care program established by the Indian government to provide comprehensive medical services to its employees, pensioners, and their dependents. Launched in 1954, CGHS covers a wide spectrum of healthcare services, including outpatient consultations, inpatient treatments, diagnostic tests, and essential medicines. Beneficiaries can avail services through a network of empaneled hospitals, clinics, and dispensaries across various cities. The scheme prioritizes affordability and quality, aiming to ensure that eligible individuals have access to reliable healthcare facilities. Rashtriya Swasthya Bima Yojana (RSBY) is a health insurance scheme in India aimed at providing financial protection to below-poverty-line families. Launched in 2008, it covers hospitalization expenses for specified illnesses and surgeries, offering a defined benefit package. The scheme is designed to improve access to quality healthcare for economically vulnerable populations by providing cashless treatment at empaneled hospitals. Beneficiaries receive a biometric-enabled smart card to avail of the health insurance benefits.

The beneficiary shall be eligible for such in - patient health care insurance benefits as would be designed by the respective State Governments based on the requirement of the people/ geographical area. • Cashless attendance to all covered ailments
• Hospitalization expenses, taking care of most common illnesses with as few exclusions as possible
• All pre-existing diseases to be covered
• Transportation costs (actual with maximum limit of Rs. 100 per visit) within an overall limit of Rs. 1000.

• Unorganized sector workers belonging to BPL category and their family members (a family unit of five) shall be the beneficiaries under the scheme.
